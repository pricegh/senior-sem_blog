Best in-class/out-of-class Experiences
Best in-class

My Computer Science education has been a big mix of anxiety-inducing, stressful assignments (or classes as a whole), and fun, engaging courses. Usually this comes down to the professors teaching it, but the course topics themselves can kind of give you an expectation of that being the case. While I have had many fun Computer Science classes, I'd say my best experience came from one of the most stressful, and that is due to the resulting relief and pride, along with great improvement in my programming skills. I'm talking about the main Y86 simulator lab for Computer Systems I. In the beginning, I was scared of being "left-behind" or in constant catch-up mode due to my inexperience with C++ and assembly. The labs were a struggle, but I grinded them out and constantly studied the code and Y86 assembly. By the end, I had transformed from a complete beginner at both C++ and the assembly language to someone with at least some confidence in my ability, and the acknowledgement of that was probably my "best" in-class experience

Best out-of-class

There is not a specific event that I can think of when it comes to being the "best" experience, but I think over the duration of the past few months especially have made me proud for being consistent when it comes to my learning. I am not 100% certain that I will be doing web development as my career over the long run, but I decided that was the path I wanted to take for now at least. So, especially over the last few months, I spent a lot of my free time learning mostly JavaScript. I started with being confused about basic HTML last July and now I am working with React, which is a more advanced JavaScript library, so the continual effort and improvement collectively is my best/favorite experience
